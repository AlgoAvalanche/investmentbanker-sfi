## VERIFIED DATA POINTS

### 1. Goldman Sachs Application Volume and Acceptance Rate
- Source: https://ophyai.com/blog/company-guides/goldman-sachs-interview-guide
- Verified via WebFetch: YES
- Data: Goldman Sachs receives over 300,000 applications annually for roughly 3,000-4,000 positions. Acceptance rate approximately 1.5-2%.
- Additional: 315,000+ applications cited in multiple search results for 2024 internship cycle for approximately 2,700 spots (~0.9%).

### 2. Goldman Sachs - Five Core Behavioral Evaluation Dimensions
- Source: https://ophyai.com/blog/company-guides/goldman-sachs-interview-guide
- Verified via WebFetch: YES
- Data: Goldman assesses candidates on (1) Commercial instinct, (2) Teamwork, (3) Integrity, (4) Judgment, (5) Drive.
- Additional: Firm's 14 Business Principles written in 1979 emphasize "client service, integrity, excellence, and long-term interests of the firm above short-term profits."

### 3. Goldman Sachs HireVue Format
- Source: https://www.careerprinciples.com/resources/goldman-sachs-interview-process (WebFetch verified)
- Data: HireVue consists of 4-6 predetermined questions with 2-minute maximum responses; candidates get 30 seconds to prepare before recording begins. Questions are "primarily behavioural and competency-based."

### 4. Goldman Sachs Superday Structure
- Source: https://ophyai.com/blog/company-guides/goldman-sachs-interview-guide (verified)
- Data: Superday consists of 3-5 back-to-back interviews, each 30 minutes, typically in-person.

### 5. Superday Offer Conversion Rate
- Source: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/superday/ (verified via WebFetch)
- Data: "The average Superday has a 30-40% offer rate and a 20-30% hire rate." Variance by location/group - unusual for conversion rates to exceed 50% or fall below 10%.
- Source 2: https://www.bankersbyday.com/investment-banking-superday/ (verified)
- Data: "the likelihood of receiving an offer in the range of 35-40%"

### 6. What Banks Evaluate - Fit Over Technical After Threshold
- Source: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/superday/ (verified)
- Quote: "once you're above a certain technical proficiency level, it's all fit"
- Source 2: https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/the-investment-banking-interview-everything-matters-but-fit-matters-most/ (verified)
- Quote: "Everything matters in an investment banking interview, but fit matters most."

### 7. Schmidt & Hunter Meta-Analysis - Structured Interview Validity
- Source: https://www.plum.io/blog/the-3-things-you-must-know-about-selection-according-to-i/o-psychologists-schmidt-hunter (verified via WebFetch)
- Data: Structured interviews have a predictive validity coefficient of r = 0.51 for job performance. Unstructured interviews: r = 0.38. Combined with cognitive ability test: r = 0.63.
- Original study: Schmidt, F. & Hunter, J. (1998). "The Validity and Utility of Selection Methods in Personnel Psychology." Psychological Bulletin. 85 years of data synthesized.
- Additional search result data point: "behavioral questions are 55% effective at predicting job performance, whereas traditional interview questions achieve only 10% effectiveness" - cited across multiple STAR method sources.

### 8. JPMorgan Superday Format
- Source: Search results from https://igotanoffer.com/blogs/finance/jp-morgan-behavioral-interview-questions (403 on fetch, but search summary verified)
- Data: JPMorgan Superday - four back-to-back interviews with analysts, associates, and one VP, each lasting about 25 minutes. Conversations blend behavioral and technical but "lean more toward fit and communication."

### 9. Behavioral Questions Proportion
- Source: Multiple search results
- Data: Behavioral questions constitute approximately 60% or more of Goldman Sachs interviews. Wall Street Prep states fit is "the most important factor that determines whether an offer is received."

### 10. Specific Behavioral Questions - Goldman Sachs
- Source: ophyai.com/blog/company-guides/goldman-sachs-interview-guide (verified)
- "Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with limited information."
- Source: careerprinciples.com (verified): "Describe a situation where you led a team. Elaborate on the process." / "Describe an obstacle you overcame and how?"

### 11. Specific Behavioral Questions - JPMorgan
- Source: search results / igotanoffer (403 on direct fetch, sourced from search summary)
- "Tell me about a time you demonstrated leadership when faced with a conflict in a team"
- "Give an example of a time you worked under pressure and met a tight deadline"

### 12. Specific Behavioral Questions - Morgan Stanley
- Source: https://www.joinleland.com/library/a/how-to-prepare-for-morgan-stanley-investment-banking-interviews (verified)
- "Describe a time you took a leadership role in a project"
- "How do you handle a situation when you disagree with a team member?"
- "Describe a time when you had to solve a complex problem"
- "What is your understanding of Morgan Stanley's core values?"

### 13. Evercore Superday Format
- Source: search summary citing Wall Street Oasis Evercore interview page
- "The superday is almost all behavioral, with three interviews with directors and one with an associate."

### 14. MIT STAR Method Guidance
- Source: https://capd.mit.edu/resources/the-star-method-for-behavioral-interviews/ (verified)
- Data: MIT recommends Situation (20%), Task (10%), Action (60%), Result (10%). Emphasizes "I" statements over "we" language. Recommends 3-5 stories in bulleted outline, not scripted.

### 15. Leland - 20 Behavioral Questions for IB
- Source: https://www.joinleland.com/library/a/mastering-behavioral-questions-in-investment-banking-interviews-tips-and-tricks (verified)
- Six core evaluation areas: Personal Qualities, Past Experiences, Technical + Soft Skills, Stress Management, Conflict Resolution, Specific Competencies.

### 16. DDI Introduced STAR Method
- Source: https://www.ddi.com/solutions/behavioral-interviewing/star-method (verified)
- Data: DDI (Development Dimensions International) introduced the STAR method in 1974.
