# Research Notes: Why Investment Banking Interview Answer

## Sources Consulted

### 1. Mergers & Inquisitions - "Why Investment Banking? How to Answer the Most Boring But Persistent Interview Question of All Time"
- URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/why-investment-banking/
- Key content: Core framework is narrative-driven, not a list of reasons. Answer should be 30-40 seconds (50-85 words), flowing from the final third of your resume walkthrough. For internship-to-full-time roles: reference two distinct work experiences, identify what you enjoyed about each, explain how IB uniquely combines both. "Talk is cheap" - cite concrete actions (classes, clubs, informational interviews) not abstract interest. Specific switching reasons: from PE (more deal variety), from VC (fewer rejections), from hedge funds (direct advising vs. following). Key insight: "This is just the last part of your story. It shouldn't even be a question if you've told your story properly."

### 2. Wall Street Prep - "Why Investment Banking? | Interview Question"
- URL: https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/why-investment-banking/
- Key content: Three elements interviewers assess - logical decision-making (skill-building, personal development, relationship-building), demonstrated industry understanding (M&A advisory and capital markets underwriting), and trade-off acknowledgment (understanding demanding nature). Strong answers include desire for "analytically challenging projects," interest in "developing a well-rounded skill set in finance," appreciation for environments that "reward competency," and evidence of work ethic. Avoid: mentioning money or prestige, sounding memorized, failing to research the specific firm, vague answers, appearing unenthusiastic about actual work. Keep under 60 seconds, maintain conversational tone, weave personal anecdotes chronologically.

### 3. Wall Street Oasis - 2024 Investment Banking Work Conditions Survey (4th Annual)
- URL: https://www.wallstreetoasis.com/forum/investment-banking/wso-2024-investment-banking-work-conditions-survey-results-4th-annual
- Key content: Average analyst hours reduced from 82/week (2021) to 72/week (2024). Peak workloads 70-90 hours per week. 72% of bankers considering quitting to avoid burnout, 51% aware colleagues planning to exit. 62% say work hours negatively affected relationships with family/friends. 22% decline in mental health, 26% decline in physical health reported. JPMorgan implemented 80-hour weekly cap with "pencils down" window Friday evening to Saturday noon.

### 4. Investment Banking Council of America - "The Truth Behind 100-Hour Work Weeks in Investment Banking"
- URL: https://www.investmentbankingcouncil.org/blog/the-truth-behind-100-hour-work-weeks-in-investment-banking
- Key content: The 100-hour myth "doesn't always match the course of most professionals in the field." Peak analyst workloads: 70-90 hours/week during demanding phases. Average workweek: 50-70 hours. M&A teams work longer than capital markets roles. Hours influenced by client deadlines and deal closure phases. Industry adapting with flexible schedules, automation of repetitive tasks, wellness programs.

### 5. Mergers & Inquisitions - "Investment Banking Interview Questions and Answers: The Definitive Guide"
- URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/investment-banking-interview-questions-and-answers/
- Key content: "Almost everyone from 'elite schools' wants to get into private equity ASAP, which runs contrary to the long-term commitment that banks are now trying to encourage." Firms now seek candidates demonstrating authentic interest in banking careers specifically, not just using banking as pathway to PE. The "why banking" answer should integrate into the larger narrative arc rather than standing as an isolated question.

## Key Excerpts for Article

- M&I: Answer should be 30-40 seconds, 50-85 words, flowing naturally from your story
- M&I: "This is just the last part of your story. It shouldn't even be a question if you've told your story properly."
- WSP: Three elements - logical decision, industry understanding, trade-off acknowledgment
- WSO 2024 survey: Average hours dropped from 82 (2021) to 72 (2024) - but peak is still 70-90/week
- IBCA: Average workweek 50-70 hours; 100-hour weeks are peak-deal exceptions, not the norm
- M&I definitive guide: Banks now actively push back against "banking as PE feeder school" mentality
- Generic phrases to never use: "fast-paced environment," "learning opportunities," "Excel mastery," "well-rounded skill set"
- Never mention: compensation, bonuses, exit opportunities, prestige

## Framework Distilled

Three-part answer:
1. Specific prior experience or action (not just stated interest)
2. What specifically banking offers vs. adjacent paths
3. Why this firm and group (if it fits naturally; omit if forced)

Context-dependent variations:
- Undergrad: coursework + internship + activities form narrative
- Pre-MBA: explain what pre-MBA career gave you + what banking specifically adds
- Career switchers: explain why IB over previous field with specific examples

Delivery: conversational, under 60 seconds, no memorized speech feel
