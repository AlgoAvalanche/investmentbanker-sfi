# Research Notes: Bulge Bracket vs Elite Boutique vs Middle Market Investment Banks

## Sources Consulted

### 1. Mergers & Inquisitions - Bulge Bracket Banks Overview
- URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/bulge-bracket-banks/
- Key content: Full-service global banks operating across all regions and service lines. Current BB list: Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan Chase, Morgan Stanley, Bank of America, Citigroup, Barclays, UBS. Key advantages: strong brand recognition and alumni networks, access to large complex transactions, competitive but lower compensation than elite boutiques. Key disadvantages: larger deal teams limit individual responsibility, increasing deferred compensation at senior levels.

### 2. Mergers & Inquisitions - Elite Boutique Investment Banks
- URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/elite-boutique-investment-banks/
- Key content: EBs specialize in M&A advisory or restructuring, competing with bulge brackets on deals typically $1B+. Prominent firms: Evercore, Lazard, Centerview, Rothschild, Moelis, Qatalyst. Key stats: "higher bonuses to junior bankers, and they offer 100% cash compensation to senior bankers" vs bulge bracket stock/deferred packages. Teams are smaller, giving analysts more responsibility. Brand recognition matters less outside finance. Some EBs "known for even worse hours" than bulge brackets.

### 3. Mergers & Inquisitions - Top Investment Bank Rankings and Categories
- URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/top-investment-banks/
- Key content: Seven-tier taxonomy - Bulge Bracket, In-Between-a-Banks (IBAB), Elite Boutiques, Up-and-Coming Elite Boutiques, Middle Market, Industry-Specific Boutiques, Regional Boutiques. Jefferies described as "the strongest middle market bank, by far." Elite boutiques "often earn more than bulge bracket banks." BB/EB analysts access "mega-fund PE" and top hedge funds; MM analysts most likely exit to "smaller private equity fund," corporate development, or lateral moves.

### 4. Mergers & Inquisitions - Investment Banker Salary Report 2026
- URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/investment-banker-salary/
- Key content: Analyst compensation data 2024-2026. Bulge bracket analyst total comp: $165-$225K. Elite boutique (Lazard, Evercore, Moelis, PWP) Year 1 top-ranked: $180-$195K. Year 2 EB top performers: ~$250K. EB associates earn "$50-$100K+ more" than BB associates. Critical distinction: EBs pay "100% cash" bonuses vs bulge bracket partial deferrals. 2024 and 2025 saw M&A fees rise 10-30% but analyst/associate comp only rose ~5%; most gains went to senior bankers. Centerview analysts push $200K year 1 (not counting $50K signing), up to $250-$275K+ by year 3, with narrow buckets (+/- $10K spread across class).

### 5. Mergers & Inquisitions - Boutique Investment Banks
- URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/boutique-investment-banks/
- Key content: Four boutique sub-categories: Elite Boutique, Up-and-Coming Elite Boutiques, Industry-Specific Boutiques, Regional Boutiques. Regional boutiques handle deals often under $50M; analysts "very few... get into private equity or hedge funds." Culture varies widely - some regional shops run 40-50 hour weeks, some demand "insane" 16+ hour days. "Elite boutiques advise on much bigger deals" vs regional firms.

### 6. Mergers & Inquisitions - Private Equity Mega-Funds
- URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/private-equity-mega-funds/
- Key content: "If you don't work in one of the top groups at a bulge bracket bank (or an elite boutique), you don't have much of a chance at these firms." Mega-fund recruiting is "fast, highly structured, and open only to select candidates." On-cycle PE recruiting contacts analysts directly at top BB/EB firms. No meaningful statistical difference in megafund access between top BB groups and top EBs.

### 7. Wall Street Oasis - Compensation Benchmarking and Centerview Data
- URL: https://www.wallstreetoasis.com/company/centerview-partners/compensation/incoming-2024-analyst
- URL: https://www.wallstreetoasis.com/forum/investment-banking/compensation-benchmarking-2025
- Key content: Centerview Year 1 analysts approaching $200K base + $50K signing bonus. Year 3 approaching $275K+. WSO forum data confirms EB pay premium over BB is most pronounced at associate level, less so at analyst level.

## Key Data Points

- BB analyst total comp range: $165,000-$225,000
- EB analyst total comp range (Year 1 top-ranked): $180,000-$195,000
- EB analyst Year 2 top performers: ~$250,000
- Centerview Year 1 all-in (with signing): ~$250,000
- EB associate premium over BB: $50,000-$100,000+
- EB pays 100% cash; BB pays partial deferred/stock
- Mega-fund PE requires top group at BB or EB - no meaningful difference between the two tiers
- Middle market most likely exit: smaller PE fund, corporate development, or lateral to larger bank
- Regional boutiques: few analysts place into PE or hedge funds
