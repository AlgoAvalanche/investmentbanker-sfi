## Key Data Points and Quotes

### Time to Review
- M&I: "busy bankers spend approximately 30 seconds reviewing resumes before making interview decisions"
- Wall Street Prep: "less than 30 seconds reviewing resumes"
- Street of Walls: "10-15 seconds" (more aggressive estimate - analysts reviewing, not HR)
- WSO search snippet: "30 seconds, max"

### Format Rules (M&I free template page)
- One page maximum for undergrads and most professionals
- Exception: Australia commonly requires 2-3 pages for entry-level
- Margins: minimum 0.5", preferably 0.75" or greater
- Font: avoid size 8; reduce font before shrinking margins
- Submit as PDF unless instructed otherwise
- Align dates right using tab stops (not spacebar)
- Center name at top in larger font

### GPA Guidance (M&I free template page)
- Include GPA even if below 3.5 - omitting it signals below 2.0 to recruiters
- SAT scores: include only if above 1400 (old scale) or 2100+ (new scale)
- No high school unless recently attended prestigious feeder or just admitted

### Bullet Structure (M&I)
- Two-part formula: (1) specifically what you did, (2) results achieved
- Connector: semicolons or "by" to link task to result
- Example language: "Valued client using DCF, liquidation analysis" beats vague descriptions
- Quantification strengthens every entry
- Project-centric or task-centric structures both valid

### Bullet Length (WSO search snippet)
- Max 2 lines per bullet; ideal is 1.5 lines
- Sub-bullets acceptable for transaction experience details

### Section Structure
- Current students: Education first
- Working professionals: Work Experience, then Education, then Skills/Activities
- Target 2-4 major work experience entries
- Skills: list languages first, then technical tools
- Avoid: "Proficient in Microsoft Office" (outdated cliche)
- Avoid: "Fluent in English" on English resume (redundant)

### What Bankers Look For (Street of Walls)
Four categories:
1. Quantitative and analytical ability
2. Initiative and motivation
3. Communication skills
4. Finance interest (clubs, coursework, personal portfolio)

Reviewer hierarchy at banks:
- Internship/analyst: banking analysts review
- Core school applicants: alumni team (analysts/associates)
- Associate positions: associates and above

### Common Mistakes
- Multiple pages (except justified by level)
- Micro fonts or excessive margin reduction
- Creative additions: photos, colors, icons, tables
- Omitting GPA entirely
- Listing 10+ activity lines without focus
- "Microsoft Office Suite" in skills
- Objective statement at top
- High school info past first university year
- "References available upon request"

### ATS
- Tables and text boxes cause ATS parsing failures
- PDF preferred for submission
- Test by copy-pasting into plain text editor

### Tailoring by Group (article original content - preserve)
- M&A/coverage: deal experience, modeling, pitchbooks
- Restructuring: distressed analysis, credit, bankruptcy coursework
- Lev fin: quantitative modeling, credit analysis, fixed income
- ECM/DCM: client interaction, presentations, capital markets
