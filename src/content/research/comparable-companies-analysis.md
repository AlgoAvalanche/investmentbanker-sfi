# Research Notes: Comparable Companies Analysis

## Sources Consulted

### 1. Wall Street Prep - Comparable Company Analysis Tutorial
- URL: https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/comparable-company-analysis-comps/
- Key content: Five-step methodology (compile peer group, industry research, input financial data, calculate multiples, apply to target). Peer group selection across three dimensions: business traits, financial metrics, risk factors. Critical rule on numerator/denominator matching for EV vs equity multiples. Median preferred over mean for groups of 5+. Common mistakes: neglecting financial adjustments, assuming market rationality, relying on comps exclusively. Practical example: $140M EBITDA company at 6.0x median TEV/EBITDA = $840M TEV.

### 2. Macabacus - Comparable Companies Analysis
- URL: https://macabacus.com/valuation/comparable-companies
- Key content: Core definition: "Comparable companies analysis involves the comparison of operating metrics and valuation multiples for public companies in a peer group to those of a target company." Peer selection sources include 10-K, proxy statements, analyst reports, Bloomberg, S&P tearsheets, Value Line, SIC code lookups. Pro forma adjustments required for material events: acquisitions, divestitures, stock repurchases, debt financings, legal settlements. Quality checks: watch for multiple trending upward (indicating declining EBITDA), negative revenue growth, margin deterioration despite revenue growth.

### 3. Damodaran / NYU Stern - Enterprise Value Multiples by Sector (US), January 2026
- URL: https://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar/New_Home_Page/datafile/vebitda.html
- Key content: Dataset covering 5,994 US firms as of January 2026. Total market median EV/EBITDA (positive EBITDA firms): 19.73x. Excluding financials: 16.95x. Range by sector: Semiconductor 34.75x, Investments & Asset Management 38.03x at high end; Oil/Gas Production 5.15x, Telecom Services 6.54x, Air Transport 7.58x at low end.

## Key Excerpts for Article

- Macabacus definition: "Comparable companies analysis involves the comparison of operating metrics and valuation multiples for public companies in a peer group to those of a target company."
- Wall Street Prep on market rationality: "the market often does indeed misprice securities," particularly those with limited analyst coverage or trading volume
- Wall Street Prep on comps as sanity check: Trading comps should serve as a "sanity check" alongside DCF, not as a standalone valuation method
- Wall Street Prep on pure-play comps: "pure-play comparable companies are non-existent," requiring flexibility especially in niche sectors
- Damodaran data (Jan 2026): Total US market EV/EBITDA (positive EBITDA firms) = 19.73x; ex-financials = 16.95x; Semiconductors = 34.75x; Oil/Gas Production = 5.15x; Telecom = 6.54x
- Wall Street Prep on five steps: Compile peer group, industry research, input/scrub financial data, calculate multiples, apply to target
- Macabacus on data sources: 10-K, proxy statements, analyst reports, Bloomberg, S&P tearsheets, Value Line, SIC code lookups
- Wall Street Prep on EV rule: "The represented investor group must match on both numerator and denominator" - EV encompasses all capital providers, equity value only shareholders
