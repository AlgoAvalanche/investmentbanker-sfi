## Verified Data Points and Sources

### Acceptance Rates

- Goldman Sachs: 250,000+ applications, 2,900 positions, ~1.16% acceptance rate (described as "lower than NASA's astronaut program")
- Morgan Stanley, JPMorgan, Barclays all "broke internal application records" in recent cycles
- Industry-wide acceptance rates under 1% at most competitive firms
- Source (verified via WebFetch): https://www.extern.com/post/investment-banking-acceptance-rate-2026

- Return offer rates: 68% at top-tier firms, 55% at regional banks
- Analyst class sizes: NYC offices hire 300-350 analysts annually; London offices 150-200
- Median GPA for hired analysts: 3.7/4.0; many Tier-1 firms enforce hard cutoffs at 3.5
- Source (verified via WebFetch): https://privateequitybro.com/investment-banking-recruiting-2024-trends-and-key-strategies/

### Interview Invite Rates by School Type

- First-round interview invites: 60-70% from target/elite schools vs. 20-30% from non-target schools
- Elite schools supply roughly 40% of bulge-bracket recruits
- Internal referrals generate 35% interview-invite rate vs. 8% for cold online applications
- Source (verified via WebFetch): https://privateequitybro.com/investment-banking-recruiting-2024-trends-and-key-strategies/

### Superday Structure and Pass Rates

- 3-6 back-to-back interviews, each 30 minutes, mostly one-on-one
- 20-35 candidates per typical bulge bracket Superday
- 30-40% offer rate per Superday (20-30% hire rate accounting for candidates receiving multiple offers)
- With 5 Superdays at 30% conversion: 83% chance of at least one offer
- Source (verified via WebFetch): https://mergersandinquisitions.com/superday/

### Interview Question Distribution

- Mergers & Inquisitions identifies four main categories: Your Story, Fit Questions, Deal/Market Discussions, Technical Questions
- Three of four categories can be prepared in 1-2 days; technical questions require weeks
- Most common technical topics: accounting, equity vs. enterprise value, DCF analysis
- "Walk me through a DCF" accounts for roughly 23% of all questions reported
- Source (verified via WebFetch): https://mergersandinquisitions.com/investment-banking-interview-questions-and-answers/

### Firm-Specific Details

- Goldman Sachs Superday: 2-5 interviews, deliberately cross-functional, structured to assess role fit and team fit; front-loads technical questions early in the process
- Morgan Stanley Superday: described as more behavioral-heavy; sometimes includes group exercises (budget allocation scenario)
- JPMorgan Superday: "rewards candidates who are operationally ready, not just technically strong"
- Common JPMorgan question: "What is a recent market event that interested you?"
- Common Morgan Stanley question: "How will recent tariffs on China impact trade deals?"
- Common Goldman question: "Share an M&A deal that interests you."
- Source (verified via WebSearch): https://igotanoffer.com/en/advice/investment-banking-superday-interviews

### Recruiting Timeline (2026 Summer Analyst / Class of 2027)

- 134 firms had posted applications as of December 31, 2025; 178 total tracked
- Networking and early ID programs begin summer of junior year (12-18 months before internship start)
- Applications open: September-November of junior year
- Superdays and offer decisions: November-February
- Elite boutiques (Evercore, Houlihan Lokey, PJT, Centerview) opened 2026 internship apps January 2025 with Feb/March deadlines
- Source (verified via WebFetch): https://www.adventiscg.com/class-of-2027-recruiting-timeline
- Additional timeline source: https://www.joinleland.com/library/a/investment-banking-recruiting

### Technical Preparation Timeline

- Minimum 2-3 months advance preparation recommended for technical questions
- Accounting basics, equity/enterprise value, and DCF are the highest-priority topics
- LBO conceptual understanding required for analyst-level interviews; full modeling test more common at associate level
- Source: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/investment-banking-interview-questions-and-answers/
