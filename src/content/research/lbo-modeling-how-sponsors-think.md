## Key Data Points

### Return Targets (Macabacus)
- Historically exceed 30% IRR for leveraged transactions
- May drop to 15-20% during adverse economic conditions
- Lower hurdle rates apply to larger deals vs. smaller ones
- Cash-on-Cash (CoC) returns: 2.0x - 5.0x typical range
- Sponsor equity target: 20-30% IRR on ~5-year holding period (Macabacus capital structure)

### Leverage Ratios
- 1980s: 80/20 debt-to-equity split
- Current market: ~60/40 debt-to-equity
- Modern lenders require minimum 25% equity contribution
- Total Debt/EBITDA: 4.5x-5.5x standard (Macabacus)
- Senior Bank Debt: typically 3.0x EBITDA, 30-50% of capital structure

### Debt Tranche Structure (Macabacus)
1. Revolving Credit Facility - floating rates (LIBOR/SOFR + premium), undrawn commitment fees
2. Bank Debt (Term A & B) - 5-8 year maturity, LIBOR-based, 2.0x-3.0x leverage multiples, maintenance and incurrence covenants
3. High-Yield Debt - 20-30% of structure, 7-10 year maturity, bullet payments, cash-pay or PIK
4. Mezzanine Debt - targets high teens to low twenties IRR over 3-5 year holds, often includes warrants
5. Seller Notes - subordinated, negotiated terms, signals seller confidence

### Coverage Metrics (Macabacus)
- EBITDA to Interest Coverage: must exceed 2.0x
- (EBITDA - CapEx) to Interest: must exceed 1.6x

### Transaction Costs (Wall Street Prep)
- Transaction fees: ~2.5% of total enterprise value
- Financing fees: ~2.5% of total debt raised

### Five-Step LBO Framework (Wall Street Prep)
1. Entry Valuation = Purchase EBITDA x Entry Multiple
2. Sources & Uses Schedule (Uses = equity buyout + transaction costs + financing fees; Sources = debt tranches + sponsor equity as plug)
3. Financial Forecasts & Debt Schedule (minimum 5-year projection, full 3-statement model)
4. Exit Returns: Exit TEV = Exit Multiple x Exit EBITDA; Exit Equity = Exit TEV - Net Debt
5. Sensitivity Analysis (base/upside/downside)

### Assumption Impact Ranking (Wall Street Prep)
1. Entry and exit multiples (highest impact)
2. Leverage multiple
3. Operational characteristics (revenue growth, profit margins)

### Value Creation Attribution (Wall Street Prep)
- Three drivers: EBITDA growth, multiple expansion, debt paydown
- Two buckets: Enterprise Value Improvement (EBITDA growth + multiple expansion) vs. Capital Structure / Financial Engineering (leverage + debt reduction)
- PE firms typically avoid relying on multiple expansion due to market volatility; debt paydown provides more predictable returns
- Add-on acquisitions and dividend recapitalizations = secondary return levers

### LBO Candidate Characteristics (Wall Street Prep)
- Strong, predictable FCFs (recurring revenue streams)
- Non-cyclical/defensive industries
- Minimal CapEx and working capital needs
- Fragmented competitive landscape (roll-up potential)
- Contractual/subscription-based revenue
- Management rollover equity as signal
- Undervalued companies in out-of-favor sectors

### Exit Horizon
- Standard: 5-7 years
- Exit routes: strategic buyer, another sponsor, IPO, recapitalization

## Framework: Entry Equity Value
Purchase TEV - Initial Net Debt + Transaction Fees = Entry Equity Value

## Framework: Exit Equity Value
Exit TEV - Exit Year Net Debt - Exit Fees = Exit Equity Value

## Framework: IRR Formula
(Ending Value / Current Value)^(1 / Number of Periods) - 1

## Framework: MOIC
Total Cash Inflows / Total Cash Outflows
