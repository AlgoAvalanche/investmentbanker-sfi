# Research Notes: Compensation Across Levels

## Sources Verified (Fetched)

### 1. Mergers & Inquisitions - 2026 Investment Banker Salary and Bonus Report
URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/investment-banker-salary/
Fetched: yes

Key data points:
- Analyst total comp range: $165K-$225K. Base: $100K-$125K. YoY: ~5% increase.
- Elite boutique analysts (Lazard, Evercore): top Year 2 performers reached $250K.
- Associate total comp: $285K-$500K. Base: $175K-$225K. Bonus: 60-70% of base in Year 1, rising to ~100%+ by Year 3.
- Elite boutique associate premium (Perella Weinberg Partners, Centerview): "$50-$100K+ more" in bonuses, predominantly cash.
- VP total comp: $525K-$800K. Base: $250K-$300K. Bonus: ~100% of base in Year 1, 200-250% by Year 3. YoY: 10-15% increase.
- Director/SVP total comp: $700K-$900K. Base: $300K-$350K. YoY: ~15% increase.
- MD total comp: $1M-$2M+ (junior MDs). Base: $400K-$600K. YoY: ~25%+ increase.
- MD bonus structure: "30-50% deferred or paid in stock."
- MD bonus insight: "Bonuses are literally percentages of fees they generate, so the sky's the limit in a great year."
- Key 2025 trend: Total revenue rose 10-20% industry-wide; junior comp up only ~5%; most gains flowed to senior bankers.
- Quote: "Bonuses do not necessarily have much to do with deal flow, reputation, or ranking of the bank" (at associate level).
- London: expect a "15-30% discount" to New York figures.

### 2. Wall Street Prep - Investment Banking Analyst Salary Guide
URL: https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/investment-banking-analyst-salary-guide/
Fetched: yes

Key data points:
- Year 1 analyst base: "$100,000 became the new standard at bulge bracket investment banks."
- Year 1 bonus: $70K-$100K. All-in: $170K-$190K.
- Year 2 base: $105K. Bonus: $85K-$115K. All-in: $185K-$205K.
- Year 3 base: $110K. Bonus: $95K-$125K. All-in: $200K-$230K.
- London Year 1: £60K base + £35K-£45K bonus = £95K-£105K total.
- London Year 2: £65K base + £35K-£65K bonus = £100K-£125K total.
- London Year 3: £70K base + £55K-£75K bonus = £125K-£145K total.
- Elite boutiques start new analysts at $110K vs standard $100K.
- Regional firms: lower base, potentially better work-life balance.

### 3. Wall Street Prep - Investment Banking Associate Salary Guide
URL: https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/investment-banking-associate-salary-guide/
Fetched: yes

Key data points:
- Associate base: Year 1 $150K, Year 2 $175K, Year 3 $200K.
- Associate bonuses: Year 1 $90K-$130K; Year 2 $100K-$215K; Year 3 $120K-$250K.
- Associate all-in: Year 1 $240K-$270K; Year 2 $275K-$390K; Year 3 $320K-$450K.
- London associate base: Year 1 £95K, Year 2 £105K, Year 3 £120K.
- London all-in: Year 1 £160K-£165K; Year 2 £190K-£215K; Year 3 £210K-£250K.
- "Bonus disparity based on associate performance grows over time."
- Stub-year associates: $30K-$40K pro-rated bonus.
- "The all-in comp is usually a bit lower at large middle-market investment banks."

### 4. Mergers & Inquisitions - Investment Banking Managing Director Careers
URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/investment-banking-managing-director/
Fetched: yes

Key data points:
- MD base: "mid-six-figure range."
- Junior MD total comp: $1M-$2M annually.
- Senior MDs and Group Heads: "well above $1-2 million, depending on closed deals for the year."
- Deferred comp: "a significant percentage of an MD's compensation is deferred or paid in stock."
- Quote: "You might lose out on the majority of your bonus(es)" if you leave after 1-2 years.
- Revenue linkage: MDs "responsible for most of its investment banking revenue" and earnings fluctuate with "closed deals for the year."
- Correction on outlier numbers: "A normal MD is unlikely to earn $10 or $20 million in one year; they would need to be much more senior or in a buy-side role for that to be plausible."

## Key Structural Themes Across Sources

1. Tournament structure: large analyst class narrows to associate, VP, MD.
2. Revenue linkage intensifies at every level upward - analysts furthest from revenue, MDs on the line.
3. Deferral increases with seniority: minimal at analyst, 10-20% at associate, 30-50%+ at MD.
4. Elite boutique premium real but concentrated: Centerview, PJT, Evercore, Perella Weinberg consistently cited.
5. 2025 trend: deal recovery boosted senior comp disproportionately. Junior comp grew modestly.
6. London discount: 15-30% below New York in USD-equivalent terms.
7. Base salary standardization at analyst level (post-2021 Goldman raise) vs. high variance at MD level.
