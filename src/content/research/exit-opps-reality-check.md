## Sources Visited

1. **Mergers & Inquisitions - IB Exit Opportunities**
   URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/investment-banking-exit-opportunities/
   - Core claim: exit opps narrative is oversold and less dramatic than mid-2000s era
   - PE still involves 60-70 hour workweeks; not a lifestyle transformation
   - Buy-side recruiting now begins "absurdly early"
   - Everyone knows about PE/HF as options - no informational edge anymore
   - The term "exit" itself is problematic - implies unidirectional movement
   - Requirements for best exits: BB/EB bank, NYC/London/HK market, target school, deal flow, modeling prep
   - Frankest quote: work involves "less grunt work" but intellectually similar - financial statement analysis bores bankers in exit roles too

2. **Mergers & Inquisitions - IB Associate Exit Opportunities**
   URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/investment-banking-associate-exit-opportunities/
   - Of Blackstone Principals analyzed: 53% came from analyst-to-PE pipeline, only 12% from associate-to-PE track
   - "More than 50% of Associates" don't reach VP level
   - "More than 10%" achieve MD status
   - Associates face fundamentally different dynamics than analysts - no structured recruiting, no headhunters
   - Associates need minimum 1 year experience before exits, often 2-3 years
   - Expect "some type of discount" on title - entering as Senior Associate rather than VP
   - Off-cycle networking required; not the on-cycle headhunter process analysts access

3. **Wall Street Prep - IB vs Private Equity**
   URL: https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/investment-banking-vs-private-equity/
   - PE base pay usually on par with IB
   - Associates "seldom receive carry" - meaningful carried interest comes at partner level
   - PE hours: typically 9am-7-9pm on active deals; weekends generally free
   - PE uses smaller teams (~15 people) with senior partner visibility
   - Key functional distinction: PE associates do fundraising, sourcing, operations, exit planning
   - IB builds models to impress clients; PE builds models to confirm investment theses with capital at stake

4. **Wall Street Playbook - IB Exit Opportunities**
   URL: https://www.wallstreetplaybook.org/blog/investment-banking-exit-opportunities-explained
   - PE first-year associates: $150-200K base + bonus, $300-400K+ total
   - Megafunds (Apollo, KKR, Blackstone): >$400K all-in first year
   - HF junior roles: $200-400K, unlimited upside for top performers
   - Corp dev: $150-250K total comp, superior lifestyle to PE
   - Startup/operating roles: $100-180K cash + equity
   - Exit timing sweet spot: Year 2 for PE and HF
   - Year 3: better for corp dev and growth equity
   - Year 4+: PE becomes harder, MBA becomes more relevant
   - Group matters: M&A/sponsors groups > industry coverage groups for PE access
   - Closed transaction experience outweighs pitch exposure for PE recruiting

5. **Web search data - recruiting timeline shifts**
   - ~70% of analysts in relevant groups recruited for PE; ~60% of those who tried got at least one offer
   - JPMorgan told incoming analysts in 2025 that accepting a future-dated job within first 18 months = termination
   - Megafunds pushing timing later; off-cycle and later-cycle recruiting carrying more volume
   - Top 5 Wall Street groups: ~50% placement into top 10 megafunds and top 10 HFs among those who pursue it
