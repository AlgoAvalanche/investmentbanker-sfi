# Research Notes: Walk Me Through a DCF

## Sources Consulted

### 1. Mergers & Inquisitions - DCF Model Guide
- URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/dcf-model/
- Key content: Three essential steps - project UFCF, calculate WACC discount rate, estimate terminal value. Terminal growth rate should be "below the long-term GDP growth rate, especially in developed countries." Focus on ranges and sensitivities rather than point estimates. UFCF = EBIT x (1 - Tax Rate) + D&A +/- Change in Working Capital - CapEx.
- Interview tip: Focus on ranges and sensitivities rather than precise point estimates.

### 2. Mergers & Inquisitions - IB Interview Questions
- URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/investment-banking-interview-questions-and-answers/
- Key content: Recommended five-step answer structure for unlevered DCF. Interviewers seek evidence that you understand why dividing analysis into explicit and terminal periods matters, and how discount rates connect to risk and return concepts. Start with UFCF formula, discount at WACC, calculate terminal value, derive enterprise value, compare to market.

### 3. Macabacus - DCF Overview
- URL: https://macabacus.com/valuation/dcf-overview
- Key content: DCF is "theoretically, the most sound method of valuation" because it is forward-looking and focuses on actual cash generation. Terminal value often represents a substantial portion of total DCF valuation. Six-step process: project UFCFs, select discount rate, calculate TV, discount to NPV for EV, subtract net debt for equity value, review and validate. Cross-checking terminal value using both terminal multiple and perpetuity growth methods is "a best practice."

### 4. Damodaran (NYU Stern) - DCF Valuation Materials
- URL: https://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar/pdfiles/eqnotes/dcfallOld.pdf
- URL: https://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar/pdfiles/eqnotes/DCFtodolist.pdf
- URL: https://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar/New_Home_Page/datafile/histimpl.html
- Key content: Five core steps - estimate discount rate, estimate current/future earnings and cash flows, estimate when firm reaches stable growth, choose right DCF model, value it. Critical warning: never mix and match cash flows and discount rates. Discounting equity cash flows at WACC produces upward bias. Current implied ERP (2025): 4.23%.

### 5. Wall Street Oasis - Walk Me Through a DCF
- URL: https://www.wallstreetoasis.com/resources/interviews/walk-me-through-a-dcf
- URL: https://www.wallstreetoasis.com/forum/investment-banking/walk-me-through-a-dcf-interview
- URL: https://www.wallstreetoasis.com/forum/investment-banking/run-me-through-a-dcf-interview-question
- Key content: Keep technical overview at high level, be ready to provide more detail upon request. 15-20 second initial overview recommended. Terminal value discussion key. If asked "walk me through a DCF" and that is it, 99% of the time they want perpetual growth, UFCF approach.
- Forum quote: "Terminal growth Rate / Exit Multiple will be the drivers to which EV is most sensitive... the Discounted Horizon Value can often make up 70%-90% of the total discounted NPV." (WSO forum thread: run-me-through-a-dcf-interview-question)

### 6. Wall Street Prep - Walk Me Through a DCF
- URL: https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/walk-me-through-dcf/
- Key content: Two-stage structure (explicit forecast + terminal value). Perpetual growth rate typically 1-3% based on GDP or inflation. Stay concise, focus on big picture. Common pitfall: rambling and losing sight of most impactful drivers.
