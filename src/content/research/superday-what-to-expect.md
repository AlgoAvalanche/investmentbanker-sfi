# Research Notes: Superday - What to Expect

## Sources Verified via WebFetch

### 1. Mergers & Inquisitions - "The Investment Banking Superday Interview"
URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/superday/
- 3-6 bankers, back-to-back 30-minute sessions, primarily 1-on-1
- 20-35 candidates per Superday at bulge bracket offices
- Estimated offer rate: 30-40% per Superday; hire rate 20-30%
- No formal case studies expected for analyst/summer analyst roles ("You should not expect a 3-statement modeling test or LBO model unless you're a lateral hire")
- Offer decisions: "a few hours to 1-2 days" after interviews
- Strategy note: With 5 Superdays at 30% each, odds of at least one offer reach ~83%

### 2. Wall Street Prep - "Superday | Investment Banking Final Round Interview"
URL: https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/superday/
- "10+ back-to-back sessions" at larger banks; schedule often deviates from plan
- First/second-year analysts ask the most technical questions
- Senior bankers focus on behavioral and background
- "Getting along well with interviewers...is arguably just as important" as technical accuracy
- Being personable can overcome technical mistakes
- Offer timeline: 24 hours to 2 days for quick decisions; waitlist if several days pass; no contact in weeks = likely rejection

### 3. Bankers By Day - "Investment Banking Superday - 2026 Guide"
URL: https://www.bankersbyday.com/investment-banking-superday/
- 5-10 rounds, each 30-45 minutes
- Assessment weighting (approximate): Resume/background 40%, Networking/fit 30%, Interview performance 30%
- Offer probability: 35-40% of Superday candidates receive offers
- Meetings span associates, VPs, executive directors, managing directors

### 4. Leland - "Superday Interview: What to Expect, How to Prepare, and How to Stand Out"
URL: https://www.joinleland.com/library/a/superday-interview
- Offer rate "typically lies between 30-50%"
- Each interview 30-60 minutes with short breaks
- Mix of behavioral, technical, market-based questions; mini case studies by role
- Resume deep-dive: every bullet point is fair game, expect follow-ups on tools used
- Interviewers compare notes - consistency across rounds is critical
- Send thank-you emails same-day to every interviewer

### 5. Wall Street Oasis Forums - Superday Conversion Rate Threads
URL: https://www.wallstreetoasis.com/forum/investment-banking/how-much-of-superday-candidates-actually-get-the-offer
- Community data: conversions generally between 15-50%, with 50% on the very high end
- "It would be very odd for more than 50% of candidates to receive offers or less than 10% to receive offers"
- No exact per-bank per-team data publicly available
- Distinction noted: offer rate vs. hire rate - some candidates receive multiple offers

### 6. IB Interview Questions Blog - "How to Prepare for Investment Banking Superday"
URL: https://ibinterviewquestions.com/blog/how-to-prepare-investment-banking-superday
- Goldman Sachs and Evercore Superdays skew most technical
- JPMorgan, Bank of America, Citi balance technical and behavioral roughly evenly
- Morgan Stanley uses group exercise and sometimes individual presentation
- Networking events and informal meals are evaluated - recruiting teams collect feedback from everyone

## Key Data Points for Article

- Candidate count: 20-35 per bulge bracket Superday
- Interview count: 3-10 depending on bank (3-6 typical for boutiques, up to 10+ at large BBs)
- Duration per round: 30-45 minutes
- Total day length: 4-8 hours
- Offer rate: 30-50% (30-40% most commonly cited)
- Offer timing: 24-48 hours in most cases
- Technical focus: heaviest from junior bankers (analysts, 2nd-year associates)
- Behavioral/fit focus: heaviest from senior bankers (VPs, EDs, MDs)
- No modeling test expected for analyst/SA roles at most banks
- Consistency across rounds is critical - interviewers compare notes
- Non-interview moments (lunch, coordinator interactions) are evaluated
