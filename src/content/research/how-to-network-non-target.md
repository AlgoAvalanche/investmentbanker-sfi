## Sources Visited

1. **Mergers & Inquisitions - State School / No Finance Background**
   URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/investment-banking-from-state-school-no-finance-background/
   - Cold outreach timeline: 3-step Saturday-Monday-Tuesday process (research, cold call, follow-up)
   - After resume refinement: response rate improved from ~0% to 50-75%
   - Key quote: "it's the person who does the work - not the school"
   - Advice: "Start earlier" even without certainty; lower expectations and let small wins compound
   - Worked 30 hours/week with 3.8 GPA - work ethic as differentiator

2. **Mergers & Inquisitions - Investment Banking Networking**
   URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/investment-banking-networking/
   - Networking is "only chance" for non-target/career-changer candidates
   - 200-2,000 emails depending on school tier
   - Realistic phone conversations: 50-200 bankers
   - Useful contacts who actively promote you: 20-30 across different firms
   - Max email length: 5 sentences
   - Call duration: 15-20 minutes optimal
   - Listen 70%, speak 30%
   - Follow up 2-3+ times; "politely persistent"
   - 3-6 months before recruiting starts = optimal timeline to begin

3. **Mergers & Inquisitions - Networking strategies/tips/templates**
   URL: https://mergersandinquisitions.com/investment-banking/recruitment/networking/
   - Same three channel structure: informational interviews, cold email, cold calling
   - Key process: mini-ask (resume review/referrals) before real ask
   - Seven-step process: find names, locate emails, request interview, follow up, conduct call, mini-ask, real ask

4. **IB Interview Questions - Cold Email Guide**
   URL: https://ibinterviewquestions.com/blog/cold-email-investment-banking-informational-interviews
   - Response rate table by connection type:
     - Completely cold: 5-10%
     - College alumni: 20-30%
     - High school/hometown: 50-85%
     - Warm introductions: 70-90%
     - Generic/poorly written emails: below 2%
   - Volume by school tier:
     - Target schools: 30-50 emails -> 8-15 interviews
     - Semi-target schools: 50-100 emails -> 10-20 interviews
     - Non-target schools: 100-200 emails -> 15-25 interviews
   - Best send times: Monday-Thursday mornings, 8-10am
   - Follow-up 1: 5-7 days after initial email
   - Follow-up 2: 5-7 days after that; acknowledge it is the final attempt
   - Junior bankers (Analysts/Associates) have higher response rates
   - Spend 3-5 minutes per prospect on research before writing
   - Five-sentence framework: personalized opening, credentials, why this person, clear ask, flexible closing

5. **Wall Street Prep - Landing an IB Interview**
   URL: https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/landing-an-investment-banking-interview/
   - Core rule: never ask for a job directly
   - Non-target strategies: cold email, LinkedIn, alumni networks, CFA Society membership
   - Attend info sessions for networking, not content
   - Positioning: ask for informational conversation about recruiting process, not a job

## Key Data Points for Article

- Non-target schools need 100-200 cold emails to build sufficient coverage (vs 30-50 for targets)
- College alumni response rate: 20-30% vs 5-10% for cold contacts
- Warm introductions: 70-90% response rate - goal of networking chain
- Realistic outcome: 15-25 informational interviews from 100-200 emails at non-target
- Goal: 20-30 advocates across firms who actively promote your candidacy
- Timeline: start 6-12 months before recruiting; 3-6 months is late but workable
- Email format: 5 sentences max; subject line with school name triggers alumni instinct
- Follow-up cadence: two follow-ups, 5-7 days apart; politely persistent
- Send time: Monday-Thursday 8-10am
- Call structure: 15-20 minutes, listen 70%, speak 30%
- Mini-ask before real ask: resume review or referrals before asking for interview intro
- Junior bankers (Analysts/Associates) reply more than senior bankers

## Frameworks Identified

1. **Tiered contact list** - Tier 1 (current bankers at targets), Tier 2 (ex-bankers in PE/corpdev), Tier 3 (adjacent finance)
2. **Alumni map** - 50-100 names before outreach begins
3. **Three-channel strategy** - informational interviews, cold email, cold calling (last resort)
4. **Mini-ask/real-ask sequence** - build relationship before making direct referral request
5. **Geographic arbitrage** - regional offices where alumni density is higher
6. **Volume-as-strategy** - non-target is a numbers game; persistence over any single conversation
